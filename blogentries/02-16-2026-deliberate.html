Deliberate

I want to talk the cycle I am stuck in and go through to try and make me more productive and feel less like an idiot for a higher percentage of my day. I really hate people who preach about "productivity" and have a million different tools and things that they use to help them be more productive. I'm being hypocritical in making this in the first place, but I really don't have an end goal of being more efficient or productive, it's just something that I found personally makes me feel better. I really hate my phone. There isn't a single time when I've been on my phone for over an hour watching videos or scrolling through Twitter, where I've felt good about myself afterward. Because of this, I have slowly been removing anything I can use to entertain myself in a simple way from my life. I've been going through this multiple month cycle where I doomscroll or do something on my phone that leaves me feeling way worse after doing it, and as a result I find a way to remove that thing from my life. Then the cycle repeats when I find something else to mindlessly scroll on. It's a slow process because I saw some things as worse than other things. A few months ago I had TikTok on my phone, and I didn't even use it that much, but I really understood short-form scrolling content to be the root of all evil, so I deleted it. I deleted instagram and found ways to remove youtube shorts from my home feed on youtube. I was left with Twitter, which I had a dedication to because of an account I run with my friends, and YouTube. I know that Twitter is equally as evil as the rest, but decided that only have one thing to scroll wouldn't be that bad. On top of this, I really felt that long form youtube content would actually be good for me, and not leave me feeling deprived. Then fast-forwarding a few months, I would literally just scroll twitter and watch youtube in place of doing productive things. Even if I didn't have anything urgent to do, and really did have this free time to spend, I still just feel terrible whenever I end up wasting it watching something. Because of this I placed barriers between me and these ways to get cheap dopamine. My youtube and twitter feed on my laptop(s) are completely blank, so everything I do has to be deliberate.

I'm writing this in two parts, I'm around a week and a half maybe after I wrote the first part and I think I'm getting somewhere. I still spend time watching youtube, but twitter is completely out of my life and I really enjoy it like that. I still tweet things, but that's the only thing I can do on there now. It's kind of nice. I will bypass the blank recommended extension because it's literally right there, but not often. It kind of defeats the whole point in wanting to be more deliberate but the stopping power is still there. I realize that removing these things from my life is only the first step to being productive, the next step is actually wanting to do things. And it's kind of hard to want to do more things after the rest of my day was spent busying myself with work. But again, I'm getting there. I'm want to find things that I like doing and can feel productive while doing. I haven't used this website in a while, but I really do want to update it and do so much more with it!! I've been really inspired recently, I like spending my free time browsing neocities websites and really exploring and reading all of these cool websites, and in a way that is a little more in depth than just quickly skimming a bunch of them. It's all honest work on those websites. I really want to change how mine looks after seeing a bunch of those other ones. Mine feels a little boring!!! I like the stars but it doesn't feel cohesive really. I'm not a really artistically inclined person (though I have tried making music before) so I'm not sure if I can easily achieve a unique well-done aesthetic. I don't even know what kind of aesthetic would fit MY personal website. I feel I would need to do some soul searching to figure that out. I really like everything being minimal so I might try organizing my website more logically. That just feels right to me even if it is a little boring. I've lost the plot again, but I'll talk a little more about some other things I want to add to the website before signing off!! I've started this thing with someone new I met where we listen to a new album every week. I want to listen to more music in general, and I mean like... I already have listened to a ton of music already I just haven't talked about it anywhere. But I really just have my outlet here and really like the idea of writing about all the new albums im listening to and definitely relistening to some albums I've already done. It's a fairly new thing I'm doing though.. and if I know myself I'm really bad at keeping up with things. So I'm going to make it a goal! I never set goals so I genuinely don't know how to do this but I'll find a way to keep myself responsible. The issue with setting goals is that I end up never feeling like I have any time to work on something or I just only feel passionate in the moment then just don't have it when I have easier things to be doing. (This goes back to the original topic of this blog). It's hard to say I'm going to set aside time before I go to sleep or something since like.. there's so much to do and writing and working on things does take time. Time is always the issue, but only in second place behind me. There's always something due for college that I have going on but obviously I don't need to work on something due a week from now especially after I've worked on other things that were due. Or something. I don't know. Okay so there's music I want to write about on here then also books! I really want to get into reading again. The last time I was into reading being like when I was a kid. Or I guess junior year of high school I got really into this one series. But either way I want to read more. I have already, but not to an extent I feel fulfilled with. I read one book, which was nonfiction for a change, titled "I Deliver Parcels In Beijing" by Hu Anyan. I've read two others before that and I'm on another book right now (also nonfiction) but I really hate this book I'm reading now. Mainly because the author is the neoliberal pedophile Steven Pinker. Probably not the best book to say I'm reading with an open mind but I really am trying to see the value in his writing and I'm just not reaching it. Even outside of who he is. Okay but either way I'm reading that book and just need to sit and get through it. More books are on the way! Finding time to read is so difficult just like everything else. I have so many things I could be doing I think I suffer from decision paralysis and end up watching a Feinberg vod instead. No offense to Feinberg.
